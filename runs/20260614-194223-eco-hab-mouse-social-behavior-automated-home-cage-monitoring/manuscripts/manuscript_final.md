### Spontaneous behavior in the social homecage discriminates strains, lesions and mutations in mice.

**Relevance:** Highly relevant as it addresses automated home-cage monitoring approaches to quantify social and other behaviors in mice.

**Methods:** The study utilized the IntelliCage system for automated monitoring of spontaneous behavior in mice over a week. Principal component analysis was applied to identify key behavioral components that could differentiate between strains and lesion models.

**Results:** Principal component analysis identified 11 uncorrelated components accounting for 82% of total variance, which characterized various behaviors such as corner visits, drinking activity, and diurnal activity patterns. These profiles were highly strain-specific and consistent across different labs and experiments.

**Key takeaways:**
- Automated home-cage monitoring can effectively discriminate between different mouse strains and lesion models based on spontaneous behavior.
- This method could serve as a prescreening tool before more specialized behavioral tests.

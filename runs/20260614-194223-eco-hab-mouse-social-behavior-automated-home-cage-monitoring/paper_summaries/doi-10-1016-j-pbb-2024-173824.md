# Clemastine improves emotional and social deficits in adolescent social isolation mice by reversing demyelination

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 3
- Reason: Reports behavioral outcomes in mice but does not focus on automated home-cage monitoring.

## Detailed Summary

This study investigates the effects of clemastine on emotional and social behaviors in mice that were socially isolated during adolescence.

## Relevance To Review

Weakly relevant as it reports behavioral outcomes but does not use automated home-cage monitoring.

## Key Takeaways

- Clemastine improves social behavior
- Demyelination is involved in social deficits

## Methods Summary

Mice were socially isolated for two weeks. Behavioral tests included social interaction and preference tests. Demyelination was assessed using MRI.

- Behavioral tests
- MRI analysis

## Outcomes

- Social interaction and preference
- Demyelination in the brain

## Results Summary

Clemastine treatment improved social behavior deficits and reduced demyelination in the brain.

- Improvement in social behavior deficits with clemastine treatment
- Reduction in demyelination observed

## Conclusion Summary

Clemastine can ameliorate emotional and social deficits by reversing demyelination in socially isolated adolescent mice.

- No automated home-cage monitoring used for behavioral assessment

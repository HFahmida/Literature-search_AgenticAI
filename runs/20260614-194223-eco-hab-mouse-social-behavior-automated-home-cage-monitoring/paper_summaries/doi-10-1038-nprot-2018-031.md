# An approach to monitoring home-cage behavior in mice that facilitates data sharing

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4

## Detailed Summary

The paper presents an automated monitoring system for home-cage behavior of mice, focusing on social interactions and activity.

## Relevance To Review

Highly relevant; the paper provides detailed methods for automated monitoring of social behavior in mice, which is central to the review question.

## Key Takeaways

- Automated home-cage monitoring can effectively quantify social behaviors in mice.
- The described system enhances data sharing and reproducibility.

## Methods Summary

The paper describes the setup and use of an automated monitoring system that records video footage from home-cages. It includes software tools for analyzing social interactions and activity patterns.

- Automated home-cage monitoring system
- Video recording and analysis software

## Outcomes

- Social behaviors (grooming, aggression)
- Activity patterns

## Results Summary

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by showing how it can be used to quantify social behaviors, such as grooming and aggression, in mice over extended periods.

- The automated system successfully quantified social interactions in mice over extended periods.
- Data sharing tools were developed to facilitate the use of collected data.

## Conclusion Summary

The described method facilitates consistent data collection and analysis, making it a valuable tool for researchers studying mouse behavior.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- The study focuses on mice; applicability to other species may vary.
- Long-term effects of the monitoring system on animal welfare are not addressed.

# Behavioural characterization of C57BL/6N and BALB/c female mice in social home cage - Effect of mixed housing in complex environment.

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: The paper uses an automated home-cage monitoring system to study social behavior in mice, which is directly relevant to the review question.

## Detailed Summary

This paper investigates the behavioral differences between C57BL/6N (B6) and BALB/c female mice when housed separately or mixed in an IntelliCage system, highlighting distinct behaviors such as anxiety-like behavior, activity patterns, and home-base formation.

## Relevance To Review

Clearly eligible

## Key Takeaways

- IntelliCage is an effective tool for monitoring social behaviors in mice.
- B6 and BALB mice exhibit distinct behavioral profiles when housed socially.

## Methods Summary

The study employed the IntelliCage automated monitoring system to observe C57BL/6N (B6) and BALB/c female mice in a social setting. Behavioral outcomes included baseline tests like open field and light-dark box, as well as activity patterns within the IntelliCage.

- Open field test
- Light-dark box test
- IntelliCage automated home-cage system

## Outcomes

- Anxiety-like behavior
- Exploratory activity
- Home-base formation
- Body weight changes
- Social interaction preferences

## Results Summary

Significant differences were found between B6 and BALB mice in baseline behavior tests and IntelliCage monitoring. B6 mice showed reduced anxiety-like behavior but enhanced exploratory activity in IntelliCage. Mixing of strains led to changes in body weight, anhedonia, and home-base formation preferences.

- B6 mice showed reduced anxiety-like behavior in open field and light-dark box tests.
- B6 mice had enhanced exploratory activity in IntelliCage during initial adaptation.
- Mixing of B6 and BALB strains resulted in anhedonia and distinct home-base formation preferences.
- BALB mice exhibited increased activity but no preference for previous home-cages.

## Conclusion Summary

The study suggests that the IntelliCage system can be used to model social behavior disorders and validate mouse models for such conditions.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- Limited to female mice of two specific strains.
- No direct comparison with traditional test methods.

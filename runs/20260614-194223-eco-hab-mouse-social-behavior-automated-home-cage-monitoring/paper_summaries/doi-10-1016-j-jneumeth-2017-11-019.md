# Between and within laboratory reliability of mouse behaviour recorded in home-cage and open-field

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 3
- Reason: Reports behavioral outcomes in a home-cage setting, but lacks automated monitoring details.

## Detailed Summary

The study evaluates the reliability of mouse behavior recorded in both home-cages and open-field settings across different laboratories.

## Relevance To Review

Relevant, as it discusses behavioral outcomes in a home-cage setting, which is part of automated monitoring approaches for social behavior.

## Key Takeaways

- Behavioral measures are more consistent within laboratories than between them.

## Methods Summary

Mice were observed in both home-cage and open-field settings; however, specific automated monitoring tools are not detailed.

- Observation of mouse behavior in home-cages and open-fields

## Outcomes

- Reliability of behavioral measures across different settings and laboratories

## Results Summary

Behavioral measures showed good reliability between home-cages and open-field tests within the same laboratory but less so between different laboratories.

- Behavioral measures showed good reliability within the same laboratory but less so between different laboratories.

## Conclusion Summary

The study highlights the importance of standardizing behavioral assessment methods to ensure consistency across studies.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- Lack of detailed automated monitoring tools and specific protocols used for observation

- Specific details on the automated monitoring systems used

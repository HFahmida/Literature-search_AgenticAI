## Methods Summary

Automated home-cage monitoring was used to observe and quantify social interactions in mice. The study focused on behavior patterns indicative of social abnormalities in the context of Rett syndrome.

- Automated home-cage monitoring

## Outcomes

## Results Summary

The results showed significant alterations in social interaction patterns, including reduced sociability and increased repetitive behaviors, compared to wild-type controls.

- Significant alterations in social interaction patterns observed in Rett syndrome mouse models.

Automated home-cage monitoring effectively identified behavioral deficits associated with Rett syndrome, highlighting its utility for studying mouse models of neurodevelopmental disorders.

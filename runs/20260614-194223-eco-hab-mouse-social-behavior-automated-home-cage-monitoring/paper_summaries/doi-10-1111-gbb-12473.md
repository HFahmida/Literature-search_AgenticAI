# Lack of anticipatory behavior in Gpr88 knockout mice showed by automatized home cage phenotyping.

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: The study uses automated home cage monitoring to assess social and behavioral outcomes in mice.

## Detailed Summary

The paper uses IntelliCage system for 22-day monitoring of Gpr88 knockout mice, revealing altered behaviors such as hyperactivity, non-habituation, and lack of anticipatory behavior compared to controls.

## Relevance To Review

Clearly eligible as it uses automated home-cage monitoring for behavioral assessment in mice, which is relevant to the review question.

## Key Takeaways

- Automated home cage monitoring can effectively assess social and behavioral outcomes in Gpr88 knockout mice.
- Gpr88 knockout mice exhibit altered behaviors such as hyperactivity and lack of anticipatory behavior.

## Methods Summary

Automated home cage monitoring using the IntelliCage system over 4 phases: free adaptation, nose-poke adaptation, place learning and reversal, and fixed schedule-drinking. Mice were monitored for 22 days in total.

- Automated home cage monitoring using IntelliCage system
- 4 phases: free adaptation, nose-poke adaptation, place learning and reversal, fixed schedule-drinking

## Outcomes

- Behavioral patterns (habituation, same corner returns)
- Social interactions (water/sucrose preference, anticipatory behavior)

## Results Summary

Gpr88 knockout mice showed delayed habituation, increased same corner returns behavior, and lack of anticipatory behavior during water accessibility periods compared to control mice.

- Gpr88 knockout mice showed delayed habituation and increased frequency of same corner returns.
- Knockout mice did not show anticipatory behavior during water accessibility periods.

## Conclusion Summary

The study confirms previous findings on Gpr88 function and discovers a novel phenotype of lack of anticipatory behavior in these mice using automated home cage monitoring.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- The study was limited to one genetic model (Gpr88 knockout) and did not compare with other behavioral testing methods.
- Long-term effects of the automated environment on behavior were not assessed.

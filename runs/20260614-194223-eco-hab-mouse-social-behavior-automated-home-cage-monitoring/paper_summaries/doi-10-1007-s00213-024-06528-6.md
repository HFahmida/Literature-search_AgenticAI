# An automated group-housed oral fentanyl self-administration method in mice.

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 3
- Reason: The study reports behavioral outcomes in mice using an automated home-cage monitoring method, but the primary focus is on drug self-administration rather than social behavior.

## Detailed Summary

This paper introduces HOMECAGE, a method for studying oral fentanyl self-administration in mice under conditions that mimic natural social interactions. Mice showed a preference for fentanyl and increased intake over time, with consumption patterns during the dark cycle. The study highlights the importance of group-housing to reduce experimental confounds.

## Relevance To Review

Tangential methods relevance, as it describes an automated monitoring system but focuses on drug self-administration rather than direct social behavior outcomes.

## Key Takeaways

- HOMECAGE is a method for studying oral opioid self-administration in group-housed mice.

## Methods Summary

Mice were group-housed in an automated monitoring system called HOMECAGE, where they could consume fentanyl or a reference liquid. Behavioral outcomes included drug preference and consumption patterns over time.

- Mice were group-housed in HOMECAGE.
- Behavioral outcomes included drug preference and consumption patterns over time.

## Outcomes

- Drug preference (fentanyl vs. reference liquid)
- Consumption patterns during the dark cycle
- Reinforcement learning behaviors

## Results Summary

Mice preferred fentanyl and increased their intake over time, with consumption primarily during the dark cycle. They also showed reinforcement learning behaviors in response to the task schedule.

- Mice preferred fentanyl and increased their intake over time.
- Fentanyl consumption was primarily during the dark cycle.

## Conclusion Summary

HOMECAGE is a valuable method for studying oral opioid self-administration under conditions that more closely mimic human social behavior.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- The primary focus is on drug self-administration rather than direct social behavior outcomes.

- No specific details on social behavior outcomes or direct comparison with isolated mice.

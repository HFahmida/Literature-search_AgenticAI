# Automated home-cage for the evaluation of innate non-reflexive pain behaviors in a mouse model of inflammatory pain

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: The paper reports on the use of automated home-cage monitoring to evaluate pain behaviors in mice, which is directly relevant to the review question.

## Detailed Summary

This paper evaluates the use of automated home-cage monitoring for assessing non-reflexive pain behaviors in mice. It found that locomotor activity decreased and immobility increased after carrageenan injection, indicating significant pain-related behavior changes. Pharmacological intervention using indomethacin improved these behaviors.

## Relevance To Review

Highly relevant as it uses an automated monitoring system to assess social behavior (locomotor activity) in a mouse model of pain, which aligns with the review question.

## Key Takeaways

- Automated home-cage monitoring can effectively capture non-reflexive pain behaviors in mice.
- These behaviors are correlated with pharmacological interventions.

## Methods Summary

Mice were monitored for locomotor activities in an automated home-cage setting following carrageenan-induced inflammatory pain. The study compared the non-reflexive pain behaviors with reflexive pain measures and pharmacological interventions.

- Automated home-cage monitoring of locomotor activities
- Behavioral assessment using reflexive pain measures (von Frey, plantar tests)
- Pharmacological intervention with indomethacin

## Outcomes

- Locomotor activity and immobility in mice
- Correlation between non-reflexive and reflexive pain behaviors
- Effect of pharmacological interventions on pain behaviors

## Results Summary

Automated monitoring showed reduced mobile behavior and increased immobility in mice with carrageenan-induced pain, which were correlated with pharmacological improvements.

- Decreased locomotor activity and increased immobility in carrageenan-induced pain mice
- Strong correlation between non-reflexive and reflexive pain behaviors
- Indomethacin improved locomotor activity in pain mice

## Conclusion Summary

The automated home-cage approach is useful for studying behavioral analgesia and screening analgesic drugs in mice models of inflammatory pain.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- Limited to a single mouse model (carrageenan-induced inflammatory pain)

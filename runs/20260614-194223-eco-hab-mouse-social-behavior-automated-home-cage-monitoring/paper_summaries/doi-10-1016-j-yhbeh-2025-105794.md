## Detailed Summary

This study investigates how mineralocorticoid receptor deletion affects spontaneous behavior in mice using automated home-cage monitoring.

## Relevance To Review

Highly relevant as it uses an automated home-cage system to monitor social and activity behaviors in mice.

## Key Takeaways

- Mineralocorticoid receptor deletion alters spontaneous behavior in mice
- Automated home-cage monitoring is a useful tool for studying mouse behavior

## Methods Summary

Mice were monitored in an automated home-cage system for spontaneous behavior over a period of one month. The study used a mineralocorticoid receptor knockout model compared to wild-type controls.

- Genetic modification of mice to create a mineralocorticoid receptor knockout model
- Monitoring in an automated home-cage system for one month

## Outcomes

- Social interaction behaviors
- Activity levels

## Results Summary

Mineralocorticoid receptor deletion led to significant changes in social interaction and activity levels, as measured by the automated monitoring apparatus.

- Mineralocorticoid receptor deletion significantly altered social interaction and activity patterns compared to wild-type controls.

## Conclusion Summary

The study concludes that genetic modifications can significantly alter spontaneous behavior patterns in mice, highlighting the utility of automated home-cage monitoring for behavioral research.

## Limitations And Missing Information

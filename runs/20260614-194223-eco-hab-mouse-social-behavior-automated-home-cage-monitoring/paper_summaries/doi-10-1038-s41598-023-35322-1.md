# An 8-cage imaging system for automated analyses of mouse behavior

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: The paper reports on an automated home-cage monitoring system for mouse behavior, which is directly relevant to the review question.

## Detailed Summary

This study introduces a novel 8-cage imaging system with animated visual stimuli to monitor mouse behavior. It reports on the analysis of multiple behaviors in both wild-type and 3xTg-AD mice over overnight recordings, highlighting differences between the two groups.

## Relevance To Review

4 = clearly eligible. The paper directly addresses the use of automated monitoring for studying social behavior in mice, which is central to the review question.

## Key Takeaways

- An 8-cage imaging system with animated visual stimuli was developed for automated mouse behavior analysis.
- The system provided detailed behavioral profiles of wild-type and Alzheimer’s disease model mice over overnight recordings.
- AD-model mice displayed reduced acclimation, hyperactivity during the first hour of darkness, and less time at home compared to wild-type mice.

## Methods Summary

The authors developed a novel 8-cage imaging system with animated visual stimuli for automated analyses. They used ImageJ and DeepLabCut software for image analysis. The study tested the system using female wild-type mice and 3xTg-AD mice.

- Development and testing of a novel 8-cage imaging system
- Image analysis using ImageJ and DeepLabCut software
- Behavioral recordings over 22-hour overnight periods

## Outcomes

- Acclimation to the novel cage environment
- Day and nighttime activity
- Stretch-attend postures
- Location in various cage areas
- Habituation to animated visual stimuli

## Results Summary

The overnight recordings provided measurements of behaviors such as acclimation, activity, stretch-attend postures, location in cage areas, and habituation to visual stimuli. AD-model mice showed reduced acclimation, hyperactivity during the first hour of darkness, and less time at home compared to wild-type mice.

- AD-model mice displayed reduced acclimation, hyperactivity during the first hour of darkness, and less time at home compared to wild-type mice.

## Conclusion Summary

The imaging system may be used to study various neurological and neurodegenerative disorders, including Alzheimer’s disease, by providing detailed behavioral profiles.

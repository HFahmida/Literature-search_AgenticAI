# Sheltering Behavior and Locomotor Activity in 11 Genetically Diverse Common Inbred Mouse Strains Using Home-Cage Monitoring

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: The study reports behavioral and social outcomes in mice using home-cage monitoring, which is relevant to the review question.

## Detailed Summary

This study used automated home-cage monitoring to assess sheltering behavior and locomotor activity in genetically diverse mouse strains.

## Relevance To Review

Clearly eligible as it reports on automated home-cage monitoring of mice to quantify social behavior, which is central to the review question.

## Key Takeaways

- Automated home-cage monitoring can effectively capture sheltering and locomotor behaviors in genetically diverse mouse strains.
- Behavioral differences among mouse strains were observed using this method.

## Methods Summary

11 inbred mouse strains were monitored for sheltering behavior and locomotor activity over several days. Automated tracking systems recorded these behaviors continuously.

- Home-cage monitoring using automated tracking systems
- Continuous recording of sheltering and locomotor behaviors over several days

## Outcomes

- Sheltering behavior
- Locomotor activity

## Results Summary

Significant variations in sheltering behavior and locomotor activity were observed among the different mouse strains, with some showing more social interactions than others.

- Significant variations in sheltering behavior and locomotor activity among the different mouse strains were observed.
- Some strains showed more social interactions than others.

## Conclusion Summary

The study highlights the utility of home-cage monitoring for quantifying social and behavioral traits across genetically diverse mouse populations.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- The study did not include a detailed comparison with traditional observational methods, which could provide additional context.
- Only inbred strains were used, limiting the generalizability to outbred or wild-type mice.

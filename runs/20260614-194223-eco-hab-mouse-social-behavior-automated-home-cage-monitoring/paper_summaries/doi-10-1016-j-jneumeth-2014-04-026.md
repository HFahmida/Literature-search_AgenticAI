# Spontaneous behavior in the social homecage discriminates strains, lesions and mutations in mice.

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: The paper reports on automated home-cage monitoring and behavioral outcomes in mice, which is directly relevant to the review question.

## Detailed Summary

This paper investigates how automated home-cage monitoring can be used to screen mutant mice strains. Using the IntelliCage system, they found that behavioral profiles could distinguish between different mouse strains and lesion models, suggesting its utility as a prescreening tool before more specialized tests.

## Relevance To Review

Highly relevant as it addresses automated home-cage monitoring approaches to quantify social and other behaviors in mice.

## Key Takeaways

- Automated home-cage monitoring can effectively discriminate between different mouse strains and lesion models based on spontaneous behavior.
- This method could serve as a prescreening tool before more specialized behavioral tests.

## Methods Summary

The study utilized the IntelliCage system for automated monitoring of spontaneous behavior in mice over a week. Principal component analysis was applied to identify key behavioral components that could differentiate between strains and lesion models.

- IntelliCage automated home-cage monitoring system
- Principal component analysis

## Outcomes

- Behavioral profiles based on principal components
- Strain and lesion model discrimination

## Results Summary

Principal component analysis identified 11 uncorrelated components accounting for 82% of total variance, which characterized various behaviors such as corner visits, drinking activity, and diurnal activity patterns. These profiles were highly strain-specific and consistent across different labs and experiments.

- 11 uncorrelated behavioral components identified, accounting for 82% of total variance.
- Highly strain-specific and consistent across different labs and experiments.

## Conclusion Summary

The monitoring of spontaneous behavior in the IntelliCage can provide significant prescreening information for mutant mice strains or lesion models before more detailed behavioral tests are conducted.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- The study was based on historical data from multiple years, which may have introduced variability.
- Further validation with additional strains and lesions is needed.

# Oral steroids for bronchiectasis (stable and acute exacerbations).

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 3
- Reason: Eligible but limited due to lack of randomized controlled trials.

## Detailed Summary

The study searched for randomized controlled trials evaluating oral corticosteroids for bronchiectasis but found none. It suggests that while there is some evidence from small studies supporting the use of inhaled corticosteroids, no randomized trials exist to make recommendations regarding oral corticosteroid use.

## Relevance To Review

Tangentially relevant as it addresses a key intervention (oral corticosteroids) in managing bronchiectasis but lacks sufficient evidence from RCTs.

## Key Takeaways

- No randomized controlled trials were found to evaluate the use of oral corticosteroids for bronchiectasis management.

## Methods Summary

The review searched major databases using specific terms related to bronchiectasis and corticosteroids. Only randomised controlled trials were considered for inclusion.

- Search of major databases using specific terms related to bronchiectasis and corticosteroids.
- Inclusion criteria: Randomized controlled trials.

## Results Summary

No randomized controlled trials met the inclusion criteria, indicating a lack of evidence supporting oral corticosteroid use in managing acute or stable bronchiectasis.

- No randomized controlled trials were identified for inclusion in the review.

## Conclusion Summary

Due to the absence of randomized controlled trials, no recommendations can be made regarding the use of oral corticosteroids for bronchiectasis management.

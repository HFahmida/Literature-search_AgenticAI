# Inhaled fluticasone reduces sputum inflammatory indices in severe bronchiectasis.

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: The study evaluates the effects of inhaled fluticasone on bronchiectasis, which is relevant to the review objective.

## Detailed Summary

The study found that inhaled fluticasone significantly reduced sputum inflammatory markers in severe bronchiectasis patients over a four-week period, suggesting potential benefits for managing the condition through corticosteroid therapy.

## Relevance To Review

Clearly eligible

## Key Takeaways

- Inhaled fluticasone reduces sputum inflammatory markers in severe bronchiectasis patients.
- Further large-scale and long-term studies are needed to evaluate the effects of inhaled steroid therapy on bronchiectasis.

## Methods Summary

A double-blind, placebo-controlled trial with 24 participants (12 receiving fluticasone and 12 receiving placebo) was conducted to assess the effects of inhaled fluticasone on sputum inflammatory indices over a four-week period.

- Randomized controlled trial design
- Double-blind study

## Outcomes

- Sputum leukocyte density
- Bacterial densities
- Concentrations of IL-1beta, IL-8, TNF-alpha, and LTB4

## Results Summary

Fluticasone treatment led to significant reductions in sputum leukocyte density, bacterial densities, and concentrations of IL-1beta, IL-8, TNF-alpha, and LTB4 compared to the placebo group.

- Significant reductions in sputum inflammatory markers with fluticasone treatment compared to placebo.

## Conclusion Summary

High-dose fluticasone is effective in reducing sputum inflammatory indices in severe bronchiectasis patients, indicating potential benefits for managing the condition through corticosteroid therapy.

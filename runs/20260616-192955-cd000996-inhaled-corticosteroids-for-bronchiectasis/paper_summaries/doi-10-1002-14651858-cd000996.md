# Inhaled steroids for bronchiectasis.

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: Directly relevant to the CLEF TAR topic on inhaled corticosteroids for bronchiectasis.

## Detailed Summary

This study reviewed two randomized controlled trials involving a total of 54 patients with radiographic evidence of bronchiectasis. The primary outcomes included lung function measures such as FEV1, FVC, PEFR, RV, and DLco. While there was a trend towards improved lung function, the short duration (4 and 6 weeks) and small sample size did not provide clear evidence to support regular use of inhaled corticosteroids for symptom control or disease progression.

## Relevance To Review

Central paper

## Key Takeaways

- Inhaled corticosteroids may improve lung function trends but lack clear evidence for symptom control or disease progression in bronchiectasis.
- Further research with larger sample sizes and longer durations is needed.

## Methods Summary

The review included only two randomized double-blind controlled trials. Data was extracted by one reviewer, with continuous outcomes analyzed as effect sizes (weighted mean difference or standardised mean difference).

- Searches of Cochrane Airways Group RCT register and Cochrane Controlled Clinical Trials Register.
- Bibliographies of included studies searched for additional trials.
- Pharmaceutical companies contacted.

## Outcomes

- FEV1
- FVC
- PEFR
- RV
- DLco

## Results Summary

Inhaled corticosteroids showed a trend towards improving lung function measures but did not produce significant effects on the primary outcomes.

- Trend towards improved lung function measures but no significant effects on primary outcomes.
- Studies too short and small to provide clear evidence for practice.

## Conclusion Summary

The available studies were too short and too small to provide clear evidence for the use of inhaled corticosteroids in bronchiectasis. Larger and longer studies are needed to assess their impact on disease progression, exacerbation frequency, hospitalizations, and health status.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- Short duration of studies, small sample size

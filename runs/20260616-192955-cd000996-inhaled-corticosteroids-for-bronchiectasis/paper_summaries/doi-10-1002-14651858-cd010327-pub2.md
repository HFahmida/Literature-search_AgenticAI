# Combination inhaled corticosteroids and long-acting beta2-agonists for children and adults with bronchiectasis.

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 3
- Reason: The study evaluates the effects of inhaled corticosteroids combined with long-acting beta2-agonists for bronchiectasis, which is relevant to the review topic.

## Detailed Summary

The study found that combination ICS-LABA improved dyspnoea and cough-free days compared to high-dose ICS but did not show significant differences in quality of life or lung function. The evidence was assessed as low quality due to the single trial with a high risk of bias.

## Relevance To Review

4

## Key Takeaways

- Combination ICS-LABA improves dyspnoea and cough-free days in stable state for adults with bronchiectasis without co-existent asthma.
- The evidence is limited by a single trial with high risk of bias.

## Methods Summary

The Cochrane Airways Group searched relevant databases for RCTs comparing combined ICS and LABA with control groups (placebo, no treatment, or ICS monotherapy) in bronchiectasis patients. One RCT was included, which compared combination therapy with high-dose ICS.

- Search of Cochrane Airways Group Specialised Register of Trials, CENTRAL, MEDLINE, EMBASE and other databases.
- Inclusion criteria: RCTs comparing combined ICS and LABA with control groups in bronchiectasis patients not related to cystic fibrosis.

## Outcomes

- Dyspnoea
- Cough-free days
- Quality of life
- Number of hospitalisations
- Lung function (FEV1, FVC)

## Results Summary

In adults without co-existent asthma during stable state, those receiving combined ICS-LABA had better dyspnoea and cough-free days than those on high-dose ICS.

- Significantly better transition dyspnoea index and cough-free days in the ICS-LABA group compared to high-dose ICS.
- No significant difference in quality of life or lung function.

## Conclusion Summary

The review suggests that inhaled corticosteroids combined with long-acting beta2-agonists may improve symptoms in adults with bronchiectasis without co-existent asthma, but the evidence is of low quality due to a single trial with high risk of bias.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- Single trial with high risk of bias limits the strength of evidence.
- No data provided for or against ICS-LABA use in adults with bronchiectasis during an acute exacerbation or in children.

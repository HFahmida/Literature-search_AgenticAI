# Role of inhaled corticosteroids in reducing exacerbations in bronchiectasis patients with blood eosinophilia pooled post-hoc analysis of 2 randomized clinical trials.

- Relevance score: 4

## Detailed Summary

A pooled post-hoc analysis was conducted on two randomized controlled trials to evaluate the efficacy of inhaled corticosteroids for reducing exacerbations in bronchiectasis patients with elevated blood eosinophils. The study found a significant reduction in exacerbation frequency and severity among treated patients.

## Relevance To Review

4 - Clearly eligible

## Key Takeaways

- Inhaled corticosteroids are effective in reducing exacerbations among bronchiectasis patients with elevated blood eosinophils.

## Methods Summary

The analysis combined data from two randomized clinical trials, focusing on patients with bronchiectasis and blood eosinophilia. Inhaled corticosteroids were compared against placebo or no treatment.

- Pooled post-hoc analysis of two randomized clinical trials

## Outcomes

- Exacerbation frequency and severity

## Results Summary

Patients receiving inhaled corticosteroids showed a significant reduction in exacerbation frequency (p < 0.05) and severity compared to the control group.

- Significant reduction in exacerbation frequency (p < 0.05) among patients receiving inhaled corticosteroids.

## Conclusion Summary

Inhaled corticosteroids can be beneficial for reducing exacerbations in bronchiectasis patients with blood eosinophilia, as evidenced by this pooled post-hoc analysis of two randomized clinical trials.

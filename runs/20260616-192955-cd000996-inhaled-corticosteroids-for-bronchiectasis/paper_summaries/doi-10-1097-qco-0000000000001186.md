## Detailed Summary

The paper reviews anti-inflammatory therapies for bronchiectasis, highlighting inhaled corticosteroids as commonly used despite limited evidence. Macrolides are noted to reduce exacerbations and improve quality of life. Brensocatib, a new inhibitor, showed promising results in reducing exacerbation frequency and improving lung function.

## Key Takeaways

- Inhaled corticosteroids are commonly used but not routinely recommended for bronchiectasis.
- Macrolides have shown benefits in reducing exacerbations and improving quality of life.
- Brensocatib, a new anti-inflammatory therapy, demonstrated positive outcomes.

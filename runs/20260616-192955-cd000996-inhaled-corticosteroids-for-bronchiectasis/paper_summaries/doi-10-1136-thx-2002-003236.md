# Inhaled fluticasone in bronchiectasis: a 12 month study.

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: The study evaluates the efficacy of inhaled corticosteroids (ICS) for bronchiectasis, which is directly relevant to the review objective.

## Detailed Summary

A randomized controlled trial evaluated the efficacy of inhaled fluticasone for bronchiectasis. Patients showed improved 24-hour sputum volume but not exacerbation frequency compared to placebo. The treatment was particularly beneficial for those with Pseudomonas aeruginosa infection.

## Relevance To Review

Central paper

## Key Takeaways

- Fluticasone improves sputum volume in bronchiectasis patients.
- Benefit is more pronounced in patients with Pseudomonas aeruginosa infection.

## Methods Summary

86 patients were randomized to receive fluticasone or placebo in a double-blind fashion over 52 weeks, with 35 and 38 completing the study. Sputum volume was measured as the primary outcome.

- Randomization
- Blinding
- Long-term follow-up (1 year)

## Outcomes

- 24-hour sputum volume
- Exacerbation frequency

## Results Summary

Fluticasone improved sputum volume (OR 2.5) but not exacerbation frequency compared to placebo. Patients with Pseudomonas aeruginosa infection showed greater improvement in both outcomes.

- Fluticasone improved 24-hour sputum volume (OR 2.5, p = 0.03)
- No significant difference in exacerbation frequency
- Greater improvement for patients with Pseudomonas aeruginosa infection

## Conclusion Summary

Inhaled fluticasone is beneficial for patients with bronchiectasis, especially those infected with Pseudomonas aeruginosa.

## Methods Summary

A pragmatic, double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomized clinical trial was conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of adding low-dose theophylline (200 mg once or twice per day) to inhaled corticosteroids for COPD exacerbations.

- Randomized clinical trial
- Double-blind, placebo-controlled

## Outcomes

- Number of participant-reported moderate or severe exacerbations treated with antibiotics, oral corticosteroids, or both over the 1-year treatment period.

## Results Summary

The study did not find a significant reduction in the number of COPD exacerbations over a 1-year period when low-dose theophylline was added to inhaled corticosteroids compared to placebo.

- No significant difference in the number of COPD exacerbations between the theophylline and placebo groups (unadjusted mean difference: 0.01; adjusted incidence rate ratio: 0.99).

## Conclusion Summary

Low-dose theophylline does not support the use as adjunctive therapy to inhaled corticosteroids for preventing COPD exacerbations.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- The study did not find a significant reduction, but this could be due to sample size or other factors.

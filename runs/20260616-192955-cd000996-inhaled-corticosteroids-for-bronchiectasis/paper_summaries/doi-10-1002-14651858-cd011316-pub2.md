# Stopping long-acting beta2-agonists (LABA) for children with asthma well controlled on LABA and inhaled corticosteroids.

## Screening

- Decision: exclude
- Relevance score: 0
- Reason: The study does not focus on inhaled corticosteroids for bronchiectasis, but rather on long-acting beta2-agonists (LABA) in children with asthma.

## Detailed Summary

This systematic review aimed to assess the effect of stopping LABAs versus continuing ICS and LABA therapy in well-controlled asthmatic children, but found no relevant studies.

## Relevance To Review

0 = unrelated

## Key Takeaways

- No randomized controlled trials were found for stopping LABAs in children with well-controlled asthma on ICS and LABA combination therapy.

## Methods Summary

The review authors searched multiple databases from inception to April 2015, identified no relevant studies, and excluded adult studies due to inclusion criteria.

- Systematic review
- Search of multiple databases and registries

## Outcomes

- Exacerbations requiring oral steroids
- Asthma control
- All-cause serious adverse events

## Results Summary





## Conclusion Summary

There is currently no evidence from randomised trials to inform the discontinuation of LABAs in children once asthma control is achieved with ICS plus LABA therapy. A large, well-designed study is warranted.

## Limitations And Missing Information

- The search was conducted up to April 2015, which may have missed more recent studies.
- Only adult studies were excluded based on age, but some adolescent data might be relevant.

- No abstract-limited evidence available for detailed extraction.

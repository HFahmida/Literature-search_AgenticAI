# Comparative effectiveness and safety of inhaled corticosteroid plus long-acting β

## Screening

- Decision: include
- Relevance score: 4
- Reason: The study evaluates the effectiveness and safety of inhaled corticosteroids (ICS) for bronchiectasis, which is directly relevant to the CLEF TAR topic.

## Detailed Summary

This retrospective cohort study analyzed the effectiveness and safety of ICS/LABA FDC compared to LAMA in bronchiectasis patients. The study found comparable outcomes between the two groups, but fixed-dose combinations like beclomethasone/formoterol (BEC/FOR) or budesonide/formoterol (BUD/FOR) showed better results than fluticasone/salmeterol (FLU/SAL).

## Relevance To Review

Highly relevant as it directly addresses inhaled corticosteroids for bronchiectasis.

## Key Takeaways

- ICS/LABA FDC had comparable outcomes to LAMA
- BEC/FOR and BUD/FOR showed better results than FLU/SAL

## Methods Summary

A retrospective cohort study analyzed electronic medical records of bronchiectasis patients initiating ICS/LABA FDC or LAMA between 2007 and 2021.

- Retrospective cohort study
- Analysis of electronic medical records

## Outcomes

- Hospitalized respiratory infection
- Acute exacerbation
- All-cause hospitalization

## Results Summary

ICS/LABA FDC had comparable outcomes to LAMA, with HRs for hospitalized respiratory infection, acute exacerbation, and all-cause hospitalization. BEC/FOR and BUD/FOR led to lower risks of acute exacerbation, hospitalized respiratory infection, and all-cause hospitalization compared to FLU/SAL.

- HRs for hospitalized respiratory infection, acute exacerbation, and all-cause hospitalization were comparable between ICS/LABA FDC and LAMA.
- BEC/FOR and BUD/FOR had lower risks of acute exacerbation, hospitalized respiratory infection, and all-cause hospitalization compared to FLU/SAL.

## Conclusion Summary

The study provides evidence on the effectiveness and safety of ICS/LABA FDC for bronchiectasis, with some fixed-dose combinations showing better outcomes than others.
